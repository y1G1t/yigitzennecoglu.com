Who I am

About Me

Background

I'm Yiğit Zennecioğlu, a third-year Business Administration student at Özyeğin University in Istanbul. I grew up in Antalya, where my family is in hospitality and energy sector — a background that gave me an early understanding of operations, hospitality, and what it means to build something that serves people well.

Business, for me, has never been purely academic. It is something I have watched up close, lived alongside, and gradually begun to participate in. The gap between theory and practice is where I spend most of my thinking.

What I'm Building

I am currently developing my expertise across three intersecting areas: finance and strategic analysis, hospitality management, and digital content creation. My studies at Özyeğin have given me a rigorous foundation in areas like portfolio theory, corporate finance, and business strategy — skills I intend to apply directly in the context of the hospitality industry.

Alongside my academic work, I am involved in an internship at our family hotel chain in Antalya, where I focus on accounting, finance, and operations. I am interested in how modern business thinking — platform ecosystems, digital influence, data-driven decision-making — can be applied to an industry as human and relationship-driven as hospitality.

Content & Writing

I write about the things I find genuinely interesting: leadership, business strategy, entrepreneurship, and the mindset behind building a meaningful life. This blog is not a performance — it is a record of how I think and what I am learning. If something here resonates with you, I would be glad to hear from you.

Beyond Business

Outside of work and study, I follow Turkish football closely — Galatasaray, first and always. I am interested in fitness, lifestyle optimisation, and the habits of people who operate at a high level. I split my time between Istanbul and Antalya, and I have had the opportunity to travel across Europe, which has shaped how I think about markets, culture, and ambition.
